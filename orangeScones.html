Cranberry-orange scones

Prep: 20 mins | Cook: 25 mins
Serves 8
These Cranberry Orange Scones are a delightful addition to any breakfast or brunch menu – especially during the holidays! Cranberries and lots of orange zest add an irresistible freshness to these easy to make scones.

For the Scone

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup white vanilla chips
  • 1 tsp grated orange peel
  • 1/2 cup vanilla low-fat yogurt
  • 1/3 cup buttermilk

For the Topping

  • 2 tbsp buttermilk or milk
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp grated orange peel

Instructions

  • Heat oven to 375°F. Grease cookie sheet with shortening. In a large bowl, stir together flour, 1/4 cup sugar, the baking powder, baking soda and salt. With a pastry blender or fork, cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  • Stir in cranberries, vanilla chips and 1 teaspoon orange peel. Add yogurt and 1/3 cup buttermilk; stir just until dry ingredients are moistened.
  • Shape dough into balls; place on cookie sheet. With floured fingers, press or roll dough into an 8-inch round. Cut into 8 wedges; do not separate.
  • Brush dough with 1 to 2 tablespoons buttermilk. In a small bowl, mix 1 tablespoon sugar and 1/2 teaspoon orange peel. Sprinkle over dough.
  • Bake for 15 to 20 minutes or until the edges are light golden brown. Immediately remove from the cookie sheet; place on serving tray. Cool 5 minutes. Cut into wedges. Serve warm.
